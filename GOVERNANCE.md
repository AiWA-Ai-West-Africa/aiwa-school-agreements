# GOVERNANCE.md — Repository Governance Principles

**AI West Africa (AIWA) — School Pilot Framework**
Version 1.0 | Effective Date: 1 May 2025

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document establishes the governance principles that apply to all materials held within the
AIWA School Pilot Framework repository. It defines the values, responsibilities, and standards
that govern how documents are created, reviewed, approved, used, and maintained.

All contributors, partners, and users of this repository are expected to understand and respect
these principles.

---

## 2. Core Governance Values

### 2.1 Educational Purpose

Every document in this repository exists in service of AIWA's educational mission: to develop
literacy, publish original works by Gambian contributors, and build institutional capacity for
sustainable educational improvement. Documents must not be created, used, or adapted for
purposes that are inconsistent with this educational mission.

### 2.2 Safeguarding

The protection of children and young people is a non-negotiable principle. All participant-facing
documents — consent forms, programme materials, school agreements — must comply with the
following:

- The **UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC)**
- **UNICEF's** safeguarding and child participation frameworks
- The safeguarding policy in `/policies/student-safeguarding/`
- Applicable Gambian national legislation relating to children and education

No document may be used in a school or participant-facing context without prior safeguarding
review and approval by the designated Safeguarding Lead.

### 2.3 Cultural Respect

AIWA operates in a culturally rich and diverse context. All materials must:

- Treat Gambian cultural values, traditions, and community structures with respect
- Avoid cultural assumptions derived from non-Gambian educational or legal frameworks
- Seek input from community representatives when materials touch on cultural practices

### 2.4 Transparency and Accountability

- Document version histories must be maintained
- Signatories and responsible parties must be clearly named in all agreements
- Review and approval steps must be recorded
- Changes to documents must be tracked and justified

### 2.5 Equity and Inclusion

All programme materials and agreements must be designed with accessibility and equity in mind,
acknowledging differences in language, literacy level, and access to resources among participants
and partner schools.

## 3. Cultural Heritage and Intellectual Property

### 3.1 The Critical Distinction

This repository explicitly recognises and upholds the distinction between:

#### Modern Participant-Created Works

Works created by individual students, teachers, facilitators, or AIWA staff as part of a
structured programme — essays, stories, poems, illustrations, curriculum materials, training
guides, and similar outputs — are **modern original works** that belong to their named creators.

These works:
- May be assigned, licensed, or published under formal written agreements
- Are subject to copyright protections under applicable law
- Must have creator attribution preserved in all publishing and sharing contexts
- Cannot be appropriated, republished, or redistributed without the creator's explicit consent,
  as documented in a signed agreement

#### Communal Folklore and Traditional Cultural Heritage

Stories, songs, proverbs, oral traditions, ceremonial practices, community knowledge, and cultural
expressions that belong to **Gambian communities, ethnic groups, or the broader public heritage**
are fundamentally different in nature.

These works:
- Are **not owned** by AIWA, by any individual participant, or by any partner organisation
- Cannot be claimed, commercialised, or exclusively licensed by any party
- May be referenced, celebrated, or drawn upon for inspiration in educational materials,
  but must be clearly attributed as **community heritage** and not individual creation
- Any use of traditional cultural materials must be reviewed by the **Cultural Review process**
  described in `/governance/cultural-review/`

This distinction is a matter of ethical responsibility, cultural integrity, and legal compliance
with international frameworks including **UNESCO's Convention on the Safeguarding of Intangible
Cultural Heritage (2003)**.

## 4. Contributor Rights

All individuals who contribute to programme materials, template development, or institutional
documentation are entitled to:

- **Attribution** — Their role and contribution must be acknowledged appropriately
- **Fair Treatment** — Contributors must not be required to sign away rights beyond what is
  necessary for the educational purpose
- **Informed Consent** — Contributors must understand how their work will be used before
  agreeing to any terms
- **Access** — Contributors have the right to receive a copy of any agreement they sign

## 5. Document Governance Hierarchy

| Level | Document Type | Approval Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Governance policies, safeguarding policy | Programme Director + Board |
| Level 2 | School partnership agreements, MOUs | Programme Director |
| Level 3 | Participant consent forms, media releases | Safeguarding Lead |
| Level 4 | Programme schedules, workshop plans | Programme Coordinator |
| Level 5 | Correspondence templates, certificates | School Liaison Officer |

---

## 6. Document Review Cycle

| Document Category | Mandatory Review Frequency |
|---|---|
| Safeguarding and child protection policies | Annually |
| School partnership agreements | At each renewal or amendment |
| Participant consent forms | Annually, or upon legal/policy change |
| Publishing and attribution agreements | Upon each new publishing engagement |
| Governance documents (this file) | Annually |

---

## 7. Restricted Materials

Documents in `/contracts/executed/` contain confidential and personally identifiable information.
Access to these documents must be managed according to the Data Governance Policy in
`/governance/data-governance/` and must not be shared publicly or outside authorised parties.

---

## 8. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes regarding the application of materials in this repository — including disputes
about attribution, consent, cultural heritage claims, or agreement interpretation — should be
escalated to the AIWA Programme Director in the first instance and resolved in accordance with
Gambian law and any applicable international standards.

---

## 9. Amendments to This Document

Amendments to GOVERNANCE.md require:

1. Proposal in writing with rationale
2. Review by at least two senior AIWA staff members
3. Formal approval by the Programme Director
4. Version increment and effective date update
5. Notification to all active contributors and partners
