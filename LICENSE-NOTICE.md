## Usage Guidance for This Repository

### 1. Internal Operational Use

Documents in this repository are intended for use by **AI West Africa (AIWA) staff,
authorised partners, and designated collaborators** in the direct delivery of AIWA's
educational programmes in The Gambia and the West African region.

Internal use includes:
- Completing and executing templates for active school partnerships
- Distributing consent forms and programme materials to participants
- Adapting templates for new school pilots under AIWA oversight
- Reference by AIWA advisors, legal reviewers, and governance stakeholders

### 2. Adaptation and Reuse

Educational institutions, NGOs, government bodies, or similar organisations wishing to
adapt AIWA's templates and frameworks for their own programmes are welcome to do so
subject to the following conditions:

- **Attribution**: Any adapted document must include a visible acknowledgement:
  *"Adapted from the AIWA School Pilot Framework, AI West Africa (AIWA)"*
- **Non-Commercial Use**: Adapted materials may not be sold, sub-licensed, or used for
  commercial purposes without express written permission from AIWA
- **No Misrepresentation**: Adapted materials must not be presented as original AIWA documents
  or as carrying AIWA's endorsement unless such endorsement has been formally granted
- **Safeguarding Integrity**: Any adaptations involving participant consent, child protection,
  or safeguarding must maintain the core protections provided in the original templates

### 3. Restricted Materials

Documents in `/contracts/executed/` are **confidential operational records** and are
not available for public use, reuse, or adaptation. These documents contain personally
identifiable information and are subject to AIWA's Data Governance Policy.

### 4. Participant-Created Works

Original works created by student or teacher participants in AIWA programmes — stories,
poems, curriculum materials — are the intellectual property of their named creators.
These works are not released under any open licence by virtue of appearing in this repository.
Their use is governed by the publishing and attribution agreements held in `/contracts/`.

### 5. Traditional and Cultural Heritage Materials

Any content in this repository that references, draws from, or is inspired by Gambian
traditional folklore, oral traditions, or community cultural heritage is acknowledged as
belonging to the relevant communities and is not claimed by AIWA. Such content must not
be appropriated, commercialised, or exclusively licensed by any party.

See [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) Section 3 for the full cultural heritage policy.
