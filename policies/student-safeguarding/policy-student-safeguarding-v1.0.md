## AIWA STUDENT SAFEGUARDING POLICY

---

## 1. Policy Statement

AI West Africa (AIWA) is fully committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all
children and young people who participate in its programmes. The safety and wellbeing of
every Student Contributor is the highest priority in all AIWA activities.

This policy applies to all AIWA staff, volunteers, contractors, facilitators, and partner
school representatives who work with children as part of any AIWA programme.

---

## 2. Safeguarding Culture and Values

AIWA believes that:

- Children are creators and contributors deserving of dignity, respect, and protection.
- Participation in AIWA programmes must always remain genuinely voluntary.
- Safeguarding applies equally to physical safety, emotional wellbeing, cultural respect,
  digital safety, and creative dignity.
- Safeguarding is not a compliance exercise — it is a culture that every AIWA team member
  is expected to embody in every interaction with children.
- Children have the right to speak up, withdraw, and be heard without penalty.

AIWA's safeguarding culture prioritises:

- **Transparency** — children and families understand what the programme does
- **Trust** — children feel safe to raise concerns without fear
- **Proportionality** — activities are appropriate to children's age and context
- **Accountability** — any failure of safeguarding will be taken seriously and acted upon

---

## 3. Legal and Policy Framework

This policy is informed by:

- The **UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC)**, to which The Gambia is a signatory
- **UNICEF** guidelines on child participation and safeguarding
- **The Children's Act of The Gambia** and related national legislation
- The **Ministry of Basic and Secondary Education (MoBSE)** guidelines on school safety
- Applicable data protection principles

---

## 4. Principles

4.1 **Child-centred approach** — The interests and wellbeing of the child come first in
all programme decisions.

4.2 **Informed participation** — Children and their parents or guardians must provide
informed written consent before participation in any AIWA programme activity.

4.3 **Age-appropriate communication** — All communication with student participants must
be appropriate to their age, literacy level, and cultural context.

4.4 **Safe environment** — AIWA sessions take place on school premises in visible, open
spaces. One-to-one contact between an adult facilitator and a student must be avoided
unless a responsible third adult is present.

4.5 **No photographing without consent** — No photographs or recordings of students
may be taken without a signed Media Release Form from a parent or guardian.

4.6 **No publication without consent** — No student work may be published without a
signed Publishing and Attribution Agreement from the student's parent or guardian.

---

## 5. Code of Conduct

All AIWA staff, facilitators, volunteers, and contractors must comply with this Code of
Conduct at all times when working with or near children.

### 5.1 Required Behaviour

All AIWA personnel must:

- Treat all children with respect and dignity at all times
- Maintain professional boundaries in all interactions with students
- Ensure a third responsible adult is present whenever meeting individually with a student
- Report any safeguarding concern immediately to the Safeguarding Lead
- Challenge and report any behaviour by colleagues that appears inappropriate
- Obtain and verify all required consent forms before any activity begins
- Brief students in age-appropriate language about activities, their rights, and how to
  raise concerns

### 5.2 Prohibited Behaviour

The following conduct is strictly prohibited and constitutes a serious safeguarding
breach:

- Physical punishment or any form of corporal discipline
- Shouting at, humiliating, or publicly criticising a child
- Any form of sexual harassment, contact, or inappropriate communication with a child
- Photographing, recording, or filming a student without explicit written parental consent
- Sharing photographs or recordings of students on personal social media accounts
- Being alone with a student without a third responsible adult present
- Using personal mobile phones to contact students directly
- Offering gifts, money, or incentives to individual students
- Discussing a student's personal circumstances, family, or welfare with unauthorised parties
- Using any student work, voice recordings, or creative output for AI training or
  any commercial purpose not explicitly consented to

---

## 6. Responsibilities

### 6.1 Safeguarding Lead

AIWA will designate a **Safeguarding Lead** who is responsible for:
- Receiving and responding to safeguarding concerns
- Overseeing the implementation of this policy
- Ensuring all staff and facilitators are briefed on this policy
- Liaising with school welfare officers and external authorities as required
- Maintaining a confidential log of all safeguarding concerns and actions taken

### 6.2 All AIWA Staff and Facilitators

All AIWA staff and facilitators are required to:
- Read and comply with this policy before commencing work with students
- Report any safeguarding concern immediately to the Safeguarding Lead
- Never be alone with a student without a third responsible adult present
- Conduct themselves professionally and appropriately at all times

### 6.3 Partner Schools

Partner schools are asked to:
- Designate a School Welfare or Safeguarding Contact
- Report any concern arising from AIWA programme activities to the AIWA Safeguarding Lead
- Facilitate the distribution and collection of consent forms

## 7. Recognising and Reporting Concerns

7.1 A safeguarding concern includes any situation where a child may be:
- At risk of or experiencing physical, emotional, or sexual abuse
- At risk of or experiencing neglect
- Distressed, upset, or disclosing harm

7.2 All concerns must be reported to the **AIWA Safeguarding Lead** immediately:

**Contact:** [AIWA_CONTACT_EMAIL] | [AIWA_CONTACT_PHONE]

7.3 Where a concern involves an immediate risk to a child, the relevant authorities in
The Gambia must be notified without delay.

7.4 All concerns must be recorded in writing within 24 hours using the AIWA Safeguarding
Concern Record form.

7.5 Staff must not investigate concerns themselves or promise confidentiality to a child
who has disclosed harm. Staff should listen, believe, and refer.

---

## 8. Digital Safety and Online Communication

8.1 AIWA does not use personal social media, messaging apps, or direct messaging platforms
to communicate with students.

8.2 Where digital tools are used in programme activities (e.g. shared documents, educational
platforms), use is supervised, school-approved, and age-appropriate.

8.3 No photographs, audio recordings, or videos of students may be posted on any platform —
including personal staff accounts — without explicit written parental consent and approval
by the Safeguarding Lead.

8.4 Students' personal information, including names and photographs, is never published on
public platforms without consent.

8.5 Student creative works are not shared with, uploaded to, or processed by any artificial
intelligence (AI) tool, system, or commercial platform without separate, specific, and fully
informed consent.

---

## 9. Recording and Media Governance

9.1 AIWA's educational activities may involve audio recordings, oral storytelling, and
educational media production. These activities carry specific safeguarding obligations.

9.2 Before any recording activity:
- All participating students must hold a signed parental Media Release Form
- Students must have given their own verbal assent to being recorded
- The purpose, use, and audience for the recording must be explained in age-appropriate terms
- Students must understand they may withdraw consent at any time

9.3 Recordings of minors must:
- Be stored securely in AIWA's restricted-access archive
- Not be accessible to unauthorised personnel
- Not be used for any purpose beyond that stated in the consent form
- Not be processed by commercial AI or data-analytics systems

9.4 If a recording incidentally captures a child who has not given media consent, that
portion must not be used and should be deleted.

---

## 10. Off-Campus and Field Production Safety

10.1 Any off-campus activity — including field trips, community visits, or on-location
production sessions — requires:
- A completed and signed Field Trip Permission and Travel Agreement from each student's parent
  or guardian
- Named Production Supervisors who have been vetted and cleared to work with children
- A minimum supervisor-to-student ratio of 1:6 (or lower if required by the activity)
- A designated school representative present throughout the activity

10.2 Production Supervisors must:
- Have emergency contact numbers for all students present
- Carry a working mobile phone for the duration of the activity
- Brief students on conduct, safety, and cultural protocols before any community interaction
- Ensure no student is isolated or unsupervised at any point

10.3 Community interactions during field production activities must respect local cultural
protocols, including appropriate conduct around elders, community leaders, and knowledge
holders. Production Supervisors are responsible for ensuring students receive a cultural
briefing before any community engagement.

10.4 If a student becomes distressed, unwell, or requests to leave during an off-campus
activity, the School Representative must be notified immediately and the student must be
returned to a safe environment without delay.

---

## 11. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the AIWA Safeguarding Lead and Programme Director.
It will also be reviewed following any safeguarding incident or change in relevant legislation.
