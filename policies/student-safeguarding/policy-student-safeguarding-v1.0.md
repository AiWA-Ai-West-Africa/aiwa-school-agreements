## AIWA STUDENT SAFEGUARDING POLICY

---

## 1. Policy Statement

AI West Africa (AIWA) is fully committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all
children and young people who participate in its programmes. The safety and wellbeing of
every Student Contributor is the highest priority in all AIWA activities.

This policy applies to all AIWA staff, volunteers, contractors, facilitators, and partner
school representatives who work with children as part of any AIWA programme.

---

## 2. Legal and Policy Framework

This policy is informed by:

- The **UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC)**, to which The Gambia is a signatory
- **UNICEF** guidelines on child participation and safeguarding
- **The Children's Act of The Gambia** and related national legislation
- The **Ministry of Basic and Secondary Education (MoBSE)** guidelines on school safety
- Applicable data protection principles

---

## 3. Principles

3.1 **Child-centred approach** — The interests and wellbeing of the child come first in
all programme decisions.

3.2 **Informed participation** — Children and their parents or guardians must provide
informed written consent before participation in any AIWA programme activity.

3.3 **Age-appropriate communication** — All communication with student participants must
be appropriate to their age, literacy level, and cultural context.

3.4 **Safe environment** — AIWA sessions take place on school premises in visible, open
spaces. One-to-one contact between an adult facilitator and a student must be avoided
unless a responsible third adult is present.

3.5 **No photographing without consent** — No photographs or recordings of students
may be taken without a signed Media Release Form from a parent or guardian.

3.6 **No publication without consent** — No student work may be published without a
signed Publishing and Attribution Agreement from the student's parent or guardian.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Safeguarding Lead

AIWA will designate a **Safeguarding Lead** who is responsible for:
- Receiving and responding to safeguarding concerns
- Overseeing the implementation of this policy
- Ensuring all staff and facilitators are briefed on this policy
- Liaising with school welfare officers and external authorities as required

### 4.2 All AIWA Staff and Facilitators

All AIWA staff and facilitators are required to:
- Read and comply with this policy before commencing work with students
- Report any safeguarding concern immediately to the Safeguarding Lead
- Never be alone with a student without a third responsible adult present
- Conduct themselves professionally and appropriately at all times

### 4.3 Partner Schools

Partner schools are asked to:
- Designate a School Welfare or Safeguarding Contact
- Report any concern arising from AIWA programme activities to the AIWA Safeguarding Lead
- Facilitate the distribution and collection of consent forms

---

## 5. Recognising and Reporting Concerns

5.1 A safeguarding concern includes any situation where a child may be:
- At risk of or experiencing physical, emotional, or sexual abuse
- At risk of or experiencing neglect
- Distressed, upset, or disclosing harm

5.2 All concerns must be reported to the **AIWA Safeguarding Lead** immediately:

**Contact:** [AIWA_CONTACT_EMAIL] | [AIWA_CONTACT_PHONE]

5.3 Where a concern involves an immediate risk to a child, the relevant authorities in
The Gambia must be notified without delay.

5.4 All concerns must be recorded in writing within 24 hours.

---

## 6. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the AIWA Safeguarding Lead and Programme Director.
It will also be reviewed following any safeguarding incident or change in relevant legislation.
