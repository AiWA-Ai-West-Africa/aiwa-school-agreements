## AIWA PUBLIC SAFEGUARDING AND SCHOOL ENGAGEMENT STATEMENT

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This statement is intended for inclusion in public policies, school partnership documents,
website materials, and external communications.

AI West Africa (AIWA) is committed to maintaining safe, respectful, and child-centred
environments in all activities involving schools, students, educators, families, and
communities.

---

## 2. Commitment to Child Safety

AIWA maintains a zero-tolerance approach to:

- Child abuse
- Exploitation
- Harassment
- Neglect
- Violence
- Discrimination
- Inappropriate conduct toward children or vulnerable persons

Protecting the welfare, dignity, and safety of children is a core organisational responsibility.

---

## 3. Personnel Screening and Vetting

All personnel engaged by AIWA are subject to appropriate recruitment, screening, and conduct
standards proportionate to their role and level of interaction with children or school
environments.

Depending on role and risk level, safeguards may include:

- Identity verification
- Professional reference checks
- Criminal background or police clearance checks
- Safeguarding declarations
- Qualification verification
- Mandatory safeguarding training
- Adherence to codes of conduct

Additional screening measures may apply for personnel working directly with children or on
school premises.

---

## 4. Compliance With School Safeguarding Standards

Personnel assigned to schools are required to comply with:

- Host-school safeguarding procedures
- Child protection requirements
- Visitor and access protocols
- Health and safety measures
- Applicable Ministry guidance and public-health requirements

AIWA works collaboratively with schools to support safe educational environments.

---

## 5. Professional Conduct Expectations

All personnel are expected to:

- Maintain professional boundaries with students
- Behave respectfully and responsibly
- Avoid inappropriate communication or conduct
- Uphold confidentiality and student privacy
- Report safeguarding concerns immediately through appropriate channels

---

## 6. Health and Safety Standards

AIWA supports reasonable health and safety measures necessary to protect students, staff,
and school communities.

Personnel working in schools may be required to comply with:

- Communicable disease precautions
- School-entry requirements
- Medical fitness standards where appropriate
- Lawful public-health measures implemented by schools or authorities

---

## 7. Reporting and Accountability

Safeguarding concerns, misconduct allegations, or violations of child-protection standards are
taken seriously and may result in:

- Investigation
- Removal from school activities
- Disciplinary action
- Referral to appropriate authorities where necessary

AIWA maintains procedures for reporting, responding to, and managing safeguarding concerns
confidentially and responsibly.

## 8. Equality, Dignity, and Non-Discrimination

Safeguarding measures are implemented in a manner that respects:

- Dignity
- Privacy
- Fairness
- Equality
- Non-discrimination principles

AIWA is committed to maintaining safe environments free from harassment, abuse, and unlawful
discrimination.

---

## 9. Partnership Commitment

AIWA values collaborative partnerships with:

- Schools
- Ministries
- Families
- Communities
- Development partners

AIWA pursues these partnerships to strengthen child protection, educational safety, and
responsible engagement practices.

---

## 10. Optional Short Public Website Version

### Child Safeguarding Commitment

AIWA is committed to protecting the safety, dignity, and welfare of all children and vulnerable
persons engaged through our programmes and partnerships.

All personnel working in schools or child-facing environments are required to comply with
organisational safeguarding standards, professional conduct expectations, and applicable school
safety procedures. Appropriate vetting, screening, and safeguarding measures are implemented
based on role and risk level.

AIWA maintains zero tolerance for abuse, exploitation, harassment, or inappropriate conduct
involving children.

Safeguarding concerns may be reported through designated organisational channels.

---

## 11. Optional Public-Facing Safeguarding Notice for Schools

### Safeguarding Notice

All AIWA personnel assigned to school environments are subject to organisational safeguarding,
conduct, and vetting requirements. Personnel are expected to comply with school safeguarding
procedures, maintain professional boundaries, and support safe learning environments at all
times.

AIWA reserves the right to remove personnel from school assignments where safeguarding, safety,
or professional conduct concerns arise.
