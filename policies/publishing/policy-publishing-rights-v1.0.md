## AIWA PUBLISHING RIGHTS AND ATTRIBUTION POLICY

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out AIWA's principles and procedures regarding the publishing of works
created by student and teacher contributors in AIWA programmes. It ensures that:

- Student Contributor creators retain ownership of their original works
- Attribution is always preserved and respected
- Publishing consent is fully informed and freely given
- Traditional and community cultural heritage is not misappropriated

---

## 2. Ownership of Contributor Works

2.1 Original works — including stories, poems, essays, illustrations, and other creative
outputs — produced by students or teachers as part of an AIWA programme remain
the intellectual property of their creators.

2.2 AIWA does not claim ownership of contributor-created works.

2.3 This applies regardless of whether the work was produced during a programme session,
at home, or as part of an assignment.

---

## 3. Publishing Consent

3.1 AIWA may wish to publish student contributor works in educational materials, programme reports,
anthologies, or online resources.

3.2 No student contributor work may be published without:
- A signed **Publishing and Attribution Agreement** from the student contributor's parent or guardian
  (for student contributors under 18)
- A signed **Publishing and Attribution Agreement** from the student contributor themselves
  (for student contributors aged 18 and above)

3.3 Publishing consent must be:
- Freely given (not required as a condition of programme participation)
- Specific to the type of publication and use described
- Informed — the student contributor and guardian must understand what they are agreeing to

---

## 4. Attribution Standards

4.1 All published student contributor works must clearly attribute the creator.

4.2 Standard attribution format:
> *Written by [CONTRIBUTOR_NAME], [PARTICIPANT_CLASS], [SCHOOL_NAME]*

4.3 Attribution must not be removed, altered, or obscured in any version of a published work.

4.4 Where a student contributor has requested that their name not be used, the work may be attributed
as *"A student from [SCHOOL_NAME]"* or as agreed in the Publishing and Attribution Agreement.

---

## 5. Traditional and Community Cultural Heritage

5.1 Works that draw upon, retell, or are inspired by traditional Gambian folklore, oral
histories, proverbs, or communal cultural expressions must be clearly distinguished from
individual original works.

5.2 No traditional or communal cultural expression may be claimed as an original work by any
individual student contributor or by AIWA.

5.3 Such materials must be attributed as community or traditional heritage.

5.4 Any AIWA publication that includes traditional cultural content must undergo a Cultural
Review as described in `/governance/cultural-review/`.

---

## 6. Rights Granted to AIWA

6.1 Where a Publishing and Attribution Agreement is signed, AIWA typically receives a
**non-exclusive licence** to publish the specific work in the described format.

6.2 The creator retains all other rights, including the right to publish their work elsewhere.

6.3 AIWA does not seek exclusive or commercial rights over contributor works unless explicitly
agreed in writing with full legal counsel available to the creator.

---

## 7. Community Benefit and Revenue Participation

7.1 AIWA believes that educational publishing should generate shared value — not extract it.
Where AIWA's publishing activities generate revenue (through sales, licensing, or distribution),
AIWA commits to the following distribution principles:

- **Student contributors** who authored works in a publication shall receive a meaningful share
  of any net revenue attributable to those works, in accordance with the terms of their
  Publishing and Attribution Agreement.

- **Partner schools** that hosted and facilitated student contributor development may receive
  a programme contribution from AIWA's publishing revenues, recognising the school's role in
  enabling the work.

- **Community contributors** (Subject Matter Experts, oral history contributors, and community
  knowledge holders) whose contributions are incorporated into educational materials shall be
  recognised and, where applicable, receive acknowledgment or compensation as agreed.

7.2 Specific revenue-sharing terms will be set out in individual Publishing and Attribution
Agreements and School Partnership Agreements. AIWA will not profit from contributor works without
a transparent, agreed-upon sharing mechanism.

7.3 This principle applies equally to physical publications, digital formats, and any licensed
adaptations or translations of AIWA-produced educational materials.

---

## 8. Review

This policy is reviewed annually and upon any significant change to AIWA's publishing activities.
