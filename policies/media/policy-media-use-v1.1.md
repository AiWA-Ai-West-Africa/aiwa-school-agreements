## AIWA MEDIA USE POLICY

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the use of photographs, video recordings, audio recordings, and other
media featuring student contributors in AIWA programmes. It ensures that student contributor
dignity, privacy, and safety are protected in all media activities.

For the purposes of this policy, **"media"** may include:
- Photographs and still images
- Video recordings
- Audio recordings and oral storytelling captures
- Educational podcasts or broadcast materials
- Language documentation recordings
- Educational media produced during on-location production sessions

All of the above are governed equally by this policy.

---

## 2. Consent Requirements

2.1 No photograph, video recording, or audio recording of a student contributor may be taken
without a signed **Media and Photography Permission Form** from the student's parent or guardian.

2.2 For adult contributors (teachers, principals, guardians), verbal or written consent must
be obtained before photographing or recording.

2.3 Consent forms must specify the approved uses. Media may not be used outside the approved
scope without additional consent.

---

## 3. Safe and Respectful Photography

3.1 Photographs and recordings must:
- Be taken in open, visible settings
- Reflect student contributors in educational, positive, and dignified contexts
- Not include student contributors in situations that could cause embarrassment or harm

3.2 Photographs must not:
- Be taken in private or semi-private settings
- Show student contributors in distress
- Focus on individual students in ways that could identify them without their consent
- Include images that could be exploitative or inappropriate

---

## 4. Storage and Access

4.1 All photographs and recordings are held securely by AIWA.

4.2 Media must not be stored on personal devices of AIWA facilitators.

4.3 Media files must be transferred to secure AIWA storage promptly after capture.

---

## 5. Permitted Uses

With signed consent, AIWA may use approved media for:

- Programme documentation and evaluation
- AIWA institutional reports and publications
- Partner communications and presentations
- AIWA's website and social media (only where explicitly consented)

---

## 6. Prohibited Uses

Under no circumstances may AIWA media be:

- Shared with media organisations or press without additional specific consent
- Used in commercial advertising or for-profit materials
- Shared with third parties without documented justification and consent
- Published in ways that could identify a child's location, school, or daily routine
  in ways that could present a safeguarding risk
- **Used to train, fine-tune, or test any artificial intelligence (AI) system or
  commercial data platform, regardless of stated purpose**
