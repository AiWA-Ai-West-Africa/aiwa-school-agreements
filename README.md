## About AI West Africa (AIWA)

AI West Africa (AIWA) is a Gambian educational and publishing initiative dedicated to advancing
**literacy development**, **educational publishing**, and **contributor development** across
West Africa.

AIWA works directly with schools, educators, students, families, and institutional partners to:

- Cultivate a culture of reading, writing, and critical thinking among young Gambians
- Support the development of original, locally authored educational materials
- Create structured pathways for students and teachers to become published contributors
- Establish durable institutional frameworks for ethical educational partnerships
- Connect Gambian schools and learners to regional and international educational networks

AIWA is not primarily a technology organisation. It is an **educational, publishing, and
literacy-development initiative** grounded in Gambian values, supported by modern tools,
and governed by international standards of educational ethics and safeguarding.

## Purpose of This Repository

This repository — **aiwa-school-agreements** — serves as the **central operational
document repository** for AIWA's school pilot programmes, educational partnership agreements,
contributor permission systems, and institutional governance materials.

It is designed to be:

| Quality | Description |
|---|---|
| **Reusable** | Templates may be adapted for each new school partnership |
| **Versioned** | All documents follow a clear versioning convention |
| **Auditable** | Executed agreements and archived versions are preserved |
| **Scalable** | The framework grows as new programmes and partnerships are added |
| **Credible** | Materials meet international standards for educational governance |

This repository is a **legal, educational, policy, and programme governance repository**.
It is not a software application repository.

## Educational Mission

AIWA's educational mission is rooted in the belief that **every Gambian student has the capacity
to become a reader, a writer, and a contributor** to the body of knowledge that shapes their
community.

Our programme work focuses on:

- **Literacy Development** — Strengthening reading comprehension and writing skills through
  structured, culturally relevant materials
- **Publishing Pathways** — Creating formal, structured opportunities for students to produce
  and publish original works
- **Contributor Development** — Training teachers, facilitators, and youth as active educational
  contributors rather than passive recipients
- **Institutional Partnerships** — Building lasting relationships with schools, ministries,
  and community organisations grounded in written, ethical agreements

## Governance Intent

All materials in this repository are governed by principles of:

- **Transparency** — Document versions, signatories, and change histories are maintained
- **Accountability** — Every agreement names responsible parties and review timelines
- **Safeguarding** — All contributor-facing materials comply with child protection standards
- **Cultural Respect** — Templates and policies acknowledge and protect Gambian cultural heritage
- **Replicability** — Frameworks are designed to be applied consistently across schools and regions

See [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) for full governance principles.

## How Templates Are Used

1. **Select** the appropriate template from the relevant `/templates` or `/contracts/templates` folder
2. **Copy** the template file and rename it according to the [Document Standards](DOCUMENT-STANDARDS.md)
3. **Replace** all `[PLACEHOLDER]` values with the specific details for the school, partner, or contributor
4. **Review** the completed document against the checklist at the end of each template
5. **Approve** through the appropriate internal review process before use
6. **File** the executed copy in the designated secure records system, not in this repository
7. **Archive** superseded versions in `/archive` or `/contracts/archive`

See [PLACEHOLDER-CONVENTION.md](PLACEHOLDER-CONVENTION.md) for the full list of standard placeholders.
