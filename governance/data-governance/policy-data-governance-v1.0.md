## AIWA DATA GOVERNANCE POLICY

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines AIWA's approach to managing data and records across its operations.
It supplements the Data Privacy Policy (`/policies/privacy/`) with operational governance
detail for staff and partners.

---

## 2. Data Classification

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidential** | Personally identifiable information; signed agreements | Consent forms, executed contracts, participant records |
| **Internal** | Programme working documents not for public sharing | Draft templates, internal reports, meeting notes |
| **Public** | Approved materials for sharing externally | Approved programme overviews, public policy summaries |

---

## 3. Access Control

| Classification | Who May Access |
|---|---|
| Confidential | Designated AIWA staff only; named on a data access register |
| Internal | AIWA staff and authorised partners |
| Public | Anyone |

Executed documents in `/contracts/executed/` are classified as **Confidential** by default.

---

## 4. Data Storage

4.1 Confidential documents must be stored in password-protected or access-controlled systems.

4.2 Confidential documents must not be stored on personal devices.

4.3 Paper originals of executed agreements must be held in secure physical storage.

---

## 5. Data Sharing

5.1 Confidential information may only be shared with third parties where:
- The participant or guardian has consented to that specific sharing; or
- It is required by law or a court order; or
- It is necessary for the immediate protection of a child's welfare

5.2 All data sharing with external organisations must be documented.

---

## 6. Retention and Disposal

See the retention schedule in the Data Privacy Policy.

When records are due for disposal, they must be:
- Securely deleted (digital) or shredded (paper)
- Disposal must be recorded in the Data Disposal Log

---

## 7. Breach Response

Any loss, unauthorised access, or compromise of confidential data must be:
- Reported immediately to the Programme Director
- Documented in writing within 24 hours
- Assessed for potential harm to participants
- Notified to affected parties where appropriate
